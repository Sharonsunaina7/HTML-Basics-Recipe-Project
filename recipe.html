Ingredients:

  • Oil - 2 tbsp
  • Kaju / Cashew - 10-15
  • Red Chili / Sukhi Lal Mirch - 7-8
  • Tomato - 4-6
  • Salt - 1 tsp
  • Ghee - 2 tbsp
  • Cardamom / Elaichi - 2-3
  • Lal Mirch Powder - 1 tsp
  • Garam Masala - 1/4 tsp
  • Coriander Leaves / Fresh Dhaniya - 1 tbsp
  • Kasoori Methi - 1 tsp
  • Malai - 3 tbsp
  • Mithai Mate / COndensed Milk - 2 tbsp
  • Paneer - 250g
  • Butter - 2 tbsp

Recipe!

Take a pan, heat some oil and add sliced onions, cashews, red chillies and saute until onion turn pink.
Add chopped tomatoes and salt to taste.

Cook it for 10-15 mins until tomatoes are mushy.

Let the mixture cool down.
Now add the mixture to a blender and blend into a smooth and runny texture.

Now heat some butter in a pan and add 2-3 ilachi, along with red chilli powder, salt, garam masala.
Add freshly chopped coriander and kasuri methi for the creamy and buttery aroma.
Stir the masalas for a minute

Now add in already prepared mixture into the pan and stir it well.
Cook it for 20-25 minutes on low flame.
Stir every 5-10 mins to avoid the gravy from touching the bottom of the pan.
Cover with a lid to avoid heat splashes of the thin gravy.

After cooking for 20-25 mins, add malai/fresh cream.
Now add Condensed milk/Minute maid.
Add salt to taste, stir it well and cook for another 2-3 minutes.

Now finally add the fresh and soft paneer to the gravy.
Cook it for another 1-2 minutes.

Serve it hot with Butter naan or Basmati rice.
